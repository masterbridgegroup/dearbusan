"This one isn't a route — it's a blank page. Tell me what you love, and I'll write your Busan day around it. Below is one day I've written many times, but yours will be yours."

Choose your theme — or mix them

Theme A

Taste

Markets, one-dish master restaurants, Busan's own pork-rice soup and raw fish culture — a full day of eating like a local.

Theme B

Frame

A photographer's day: golden-hour coastlines, pastel alleys, skywalks and the shot lists Mira has built over years.

Theme C

Story

Temples above the sea, war-era history, markets born from refugees — the deep story of Korea's second city.

A sample day

09:00

Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

One of Korea's only seaside temples, built on the rocks where the waves break. Arrive early, before the crowds, when the sea air still smells like morning.

11:00

Blueline Park Coastal Train

Ride the sky capsule along the cliff line from Mipo to Cheongsapo — the coastline unrolling on one side, and nothing to do but watch.

13:00

Lunch, the local way

No tourist menus. Mira orders, you eat — dwaeji-gukbap, fresh sashimi at the source, or whatever your theme calls for.

15:00

Haeundae Beach & Dongbaek Island Walk

Korea's most famous beach, then the pine-shaded coastal path around Dongbaekseom to the lighthouse and the mermaid statue.

17:30

Gwangalli, as the lights come on

End the day on the sand as Gwangan Bridge lights up across the water. Coffee, a glass of something, and the best free show in Busan.
